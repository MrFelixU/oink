Year 8 Set 4

Autumn First Half Term (HT1) (8 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Calculations and rounding

  • Use written methods to multiply numbers with up to 3 digits
  • Use written methods to divide a large number by a single digit
  • Use written methods to complete the four operations with decimals
  • Order a set of numbers
  • Add and subtract negative numbers
  • Multiply and divide negative numbers
  • Find the square and square root of a number
  • Find the cube and cube root of a number
  • Multiply and divide numbers by powers of 10
  • Multiply and divide numbers by negative powers of 10
  • Round numbers to the nearest whole, 10 or 100
  • Round to a number of decimal places
  • Estimate calculations by rounding

Indices and expressions

  • Describe the basic rules of algebra, including a x a = a2, ab means a x b and 7a means 7 lots of a
  • Understand that a x a x a x a x a = a5 and can use index notation to simplify algebra
  • Use BIDMAS to complete a calculation
  • Describe what is meant by a like term
  • Simplify algebra by collecting like terms
  • Expand a single bracket
  • Expand two sets of single brackets and simplify
  • Write an expression to describe a simple scenario

Angles and constructions

  • Identify 3D and 2D shapes by name and count their vertices, faces and edges
  • Identify types of triangle and quadrilateral and describe their properties
  • Draw and measure angles
  • Calculate missing angles on straight lines and around a point
  • Calculate missing angles in triangles and quadrilaterals
  • Identify corresponding, alternate and co-interior/supplementary angles in parallel lines
  • Solve problems involving simple angle properties and angles in parallel lines
  • Use a compass and protractor
  • Perform simple constructions using the correct equipment (perp bisector, angle bisector, constructing triangles)

Autumn Second Half Term (HT2) (7 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Averages and data

  • Describe the difference between qualitative and quantitative data
  • Analyse questions in a survey
  • Write an appropriate question for a survey
  • Understand what sampling is and when it can be biased
  • Draw a bar chart for discrete and continuous data
  • Draw an appropriate diagram to display data from a frequency table
  • Draw and interpret a scatter graph
  • Calculate the mean, median, mode and range and choose which is the most appropriate
  • Construct a stem and leaf diagram
  • Calculate angles and construct a pie chart
  • Choose which type of representation of data is most appropriate

Factors, multiples and fractions

  • Identify factors and multiples
  • Find the HCF and LCM of two numbers
  • Write a number as a product of its prime factors
  • Use prime factor decomposition to find the HCF and LCM
  • Understand that the line in a fraction means divide
  • Recall simple FDP conversions e.g. 0.5, 0.75, 0.25, 0.1
  • Convert between fractions, decimals and percentages
  • Find equivalent fractions
  • Write a set of fractions in order of size
  • Find a fraction of an amount
  • Perform the four operations with fractions
  • Change between mixed numbers and improper fractions

Solving equations and inequalities

  • Solve a linear equation
  • Solve a linear equation involving brackets
  • Solve simple linear equations including ones with answers that are decimals or negatives or need to be left as fractions
  • Solve linear equations with unknowns on both sides including those that have brackets
  • Solve simple equations that involve fractions
  • Form and solve linear equations

Year 8 Set 4

Summer First Half Term (HT5) (5 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Probability

  • Understand that a probability can be written as a fraction, decimal or percentage
  • Calculate the probability of an event when outcomes are equally likely
  • Create a sample space diagram to list all possible outcomes
  • Describe the difference between theoretical and experimental probability
  • Understand that the more times I repeat an experiment, the closer to the experimental probability should be to theoretical
  • Find the probability that an event does not occur
  • Use the fact that probability adds up to a whole to solve problems

Sequences

  • Understand that a letter in maths represents a variable
  • Identify and apply simple algebra rules
  • Identify the term to term rule of a sequence
  • Continue a sequence using the term to term rule
  • Work out the nth term rule of a sequence
  • Use the nth term rule to calculate any term of a sequence
  • Solve problems involving sequences

Graphs and Coordinates

  • Plot co-ordinates in all four quadrants
  • Describe what makes a function linear
  • Complete a mapping diagram
  • Draw a linear graph
  • Draw special graphs such as y=5, x= -3 and y=x
  • Describe what gradient is and calculate the gradient of a line
  • State the gradient and y intercept of a line given its equation
  • Write the equation of a line given its graph
  • Draw graphs of functions in the form ax + by = c
  • Write the equations of two lines which will be parallel to one another
