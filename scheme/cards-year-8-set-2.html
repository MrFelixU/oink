Year 8 Set 2

Autumn First Half Term (HT1) (8 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Calculations, rounding and standard form

  • Use written methods to multiply numbers with up to 3 digits
  • Use written methods to divide a large number by a single digit
  • Use written methods to complete the four operations with decimals
  • Complete the four operations with negative numbers
  • Multiply and divide numbers by powers of 10
  • Multiply and divide numbers by negative powers of 10
  • Write numbers in standard form
  • Change numbers from standard form back into ordinary numbers
  • Round to a number of decimal places
  • Round to a number of significant figures
  • Estimate calculations by rounding
  • Recognise reciprocals

Indices and expressions

  • Use BIDMAS to complete a calculation
  • Simplify algebra by collecting like terms
  • Understand the difference between equations, expressions, formulae and identities
  • Apply simple index laws to algebra
  • Simplify fractional and negative indices
  • Find the reciprocal of a value
  • Expand and simplify single brackets
  • Expand and simplify back to back brackets
  • Factorise an expression by taking out common factors
  • Factorise an expression into double brackets
  • Know when to factorise an expression into two brackets and when to use one
  • Factorise using the difference of two squares

Angles and constructions

  • Solve problems involving simple angle properties and angles in parallel lines
  • Identify the sum of interior angles in a polygon given the number of sides
  • Calculate the size of and interior and exterior angle in a regular polygon
  • Use a compass and protractor
  • Perform simple constructions using the correct equipment (perp bisector, angle bisector, constructing triangles)

Autumn Second Half Term (HT2) (7 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Averages and data

  • Describe the difference between qualitative and quantitative data
  • Analyse questions in a survey
  • Write an appropriate question for a survey
  • Understand what sampling is and when it can be biased
  • Draw and interpret a scatter graph
  • Calculate the mean, median, mode and range, choose which is the most appropriate and solve problems involving averages
  • Calculate the mean from a frequency table and a grouped frequency table
  • Draw a frequency polygon
  • Construct a stem and leaf diagram
  • Calculate angles and construct a pie chart
  • Choose which type of representation of data is most appropriate

Factors, multiples and fractions

  • Identify factors and multiples
  • Find the HCF and LCM of two numbers
  • Write a number as a product of its prime factors
  • Use prime factor decomposition to find the HCF and LCM
  • Use the four operations with fractions and mixed numbers
  • Find a fraction of an amount
  • Change between fractions, decimals and percentages
  • Solve problems with fractions

Solving equations and inequalities

  • Solve linear equations with unknowns on both sides and those which include fractions and those that have brackets
  • Form and solve linear equations
  • Find equivalent algebraic fractions
  • Add and subtract algebraic fractions
  • Write numbers to satisfy an inequality
  • Show inequalities on a number line
  • Read inequalities from a number line
  • Solve linear inequalities
  • Rearrange a formula where the subject appears once
  • Rearrange simple formula where the subject appears twice
  • Solve simultaneous equations by elimination
  • Form and solve simultaneous equations

Year 8 Set 2

Summer First Half Term (HT5) (5 weeks)

Teaching Assessment

Probability

  • Understand that a probability can be written as a fraction, decimal or percentage
  • Calculate a simple probability
  • Understand what is meant by mutually exclusive and can give an example of two mutually exclusive events
  • Understand the difference between experimental and theoretical probability
  • Decide whether two events are independent
  • Calculate the probability that two events will occur
  • Draw a tree diagram to represent a problem with independent probabilities
  • Use tree diagrams to solve problems

Sequences

  • Understand that a letter in maths represents a variable
  • Identify and apply simple algebra rules
  • Substitute values into a formula
  • Identify the term to term rule of a sequence
  • Continue a sequence using the term to term rule
  • Work out the nth term rule of a sequence
  • Use the nth term rule to calculate any term of a sequence
  • Solve problems involving sequences
  • Identify when a sequence is arithmetic, geometric and quadratic

Graphs and Coordinates

  • Plot co-ordinates in all four quadrants
  • Describe what makes a function linear
  • Complete a mapping diagram
  • Draw a linear graph
  • Draw special graphs such as y=5, x= -3 and y=x
  • Describe what gradient is and calculate the gradient of a line
  • State the gradient and y intercept of a line given its equation
  • Write the equation of a line given its graph
  • Draw graphs of functions in the form ax + by = c
  • Write the equations of two lines which will be parallel to one another
  • Solve simultaneous equations graphically
